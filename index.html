Diversity and Inclusion

A comprehensive guide to understanding diversity and inclusion and why they matter in workplaces

About Us

We are an organisation that raises awareness of diversity and inclusion. We do this by running workshops, hosting webinars and doing assessments.

Overview

  • Diversity and inclusion are related concepts but they are not the same
  • Collectively, diversity and inclusion are about understanding each other, recognizing each other’s differences and making everyone feel welcomed in an environment whether it’d be a workplace, gym or some other establishment
  • Women account for approximately 42% of the global workforce (International Labour Organization)

What Is Diversity?

Diversity is concerned with the presence of a multitude of various ethnic/cultural backgrounds, perspectives, identities, and experiences within a group, organization, society or some other establishment. This can include characteristics such as age, race, religion, ethnicity, nationality, etc.

What Is Inclusion?

Inclusion is the practice of ensuring that people from all backgrounds and abilities feel welcome, and are actively valued, respected, and integrated into a group, society or some other establishment. An inclusive environment is one where people feel respected, valued, heard, welcomed and able to fully participate

Why Diversity and Inclusion Matters

  • Workplace diversity is especially important because it has been proven to increase productivity, employee engagement and innovation
  • Inclusion is essential because it ensures all individuals, regardless of their background or abilities, feel valued, respected, and empowered to participate
  • Companies with diverse executive teams are about 39% more likely to outperform financially (McKinsey & Company, Diversity Matters Even More, 2023)
  • Diverse teams tend to make better decisions because they bring a wider range of perspectives and experiences
  • Inclusive workplaces are associated with higher employee engagement, improved collaboration, and lower turnover

Common Examples

Diversity

  • Employing people of different races and ethnicities
  • Having employees from various age groups
  • Having employees from different religions

Inclusion

  • Ensuring all employees have equal opportunities
  • Providing accommodations for employees with disabilities
  • Providing mentorship programs for employees from diverse backgrounds

Summary

  • Diversity: Valuing differences in backgrounds, identities, experiences, perspectives, and ways of thinking
  • Inclusion: Creating an environment where everyone feels respected, welcomed, and able to participate fully
  • Equity: Ensuring fair access to opportunities by recognizing and addressing different needs and barriers
  • Respect and Belonging: Fostering a culture where people feel accepted, valued, and safe to be themselves
  • Accessibility and Equal Opportunity: Removing barriers and providing fair access to participation, development, and success
  • Awareness and Collaboration: Encouraging respectful communication, reducing bias, and valuing diverse perspectives to strengthen teamwork and decision-making
